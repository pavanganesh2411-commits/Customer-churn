## 🎯 Project Objective
To identify key factors contributing to customer churn and provide actionable insights that help banks improve customer retention and reduce revenue loss.
## 📊 Key Insights

- Customers in the **51–60 age group** show the highest churn rate compared to other age segments.

- Customers with a **credit score below 400** are more likely to churn than customers with higher credit scores.

- Customers with account balances in the **1K–10K range** demonstrate higher churn rates than other balance groups.

- Approximately **51% of customers are inactive**, indicating a significant risk factor associated with customer churn.

- The majority of customers are associated with **Product 1**, making it the most widely used product among customers.

 ## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Develop targeted retention programs for customers in the **51–60 age group**, including personalized financial support and loyalty benefits.

- Implement risk-based monitoring and offer tailored retention incentives for customers with **credit scores below 400**, as they show higher churn probability.

- Introduce customized offers and engagement strategies for customers with **balances between 1K–10K**, as this group shows higher churn tendencies.

- Launch re-engagement campaigns such as reminders, offers, and digital incentives to activate the **51% inactive customers**.

- Enhance and promote **Product 1** features with bundled services and loyalty rewards to increase customer satisfaction and retention.
